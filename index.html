Tarpaulin Ticketing System

Synthesis of the Practicum Engagement

My internship at Biñan City Hall was a meaningful opportunity to apply and expand my existing skills in a real-world environment. Before the internship, I had already started working with CodeIgniter 4, so this project allowed me to further sharpen my understanding of the framework and apply it in a more structured, team-based setting. I deepened my knowledge of PHP development and became more confident in configuring and working with Apache for local hosting and deployment. As part of the team behind the Tarpaulin Ticketing System, I took on responsibilities in backend development, database integration, and feature implementation. I particularly enjoyed experimenting with Bootstrap to design user-friendly and responsive interfaces—it gave me the freedom to explore creative layouts while still adhering to professional standards. I also learned how to use AJAX for dynamic content updates and implemented WebSocket to support real-time functionalities like a live chat modal and auto-refreshing DataTables, which greatly improved the system’s responsiveness and interactivity.

The internship also gave me a clearer view of how government workflows function, especially the request and approval process for printing tarpaulins for public events. I gained experience in collaborating with a team, following coding best practices, and documenting systems thoroughly. Just as importantly, I learned how to work with end-users—gathering their feedback, understanding their pain points, and improving the system based on their input. It was a well-rounded experience that helped me grow both technically and professionally.

SafeStep

Mobile Application

SafeStep is a mobile-based navigation solution created to support visually impaired individuals in moving safely and independently. The system combines real-time object detection using smartphone cameras, GPS for accurate tracking, and intuitive multi-sensory alerts through voice and vibration. Enhanced by IoT hardware like ultrasonic sensors and an ESP32-CAM, it detects nearby obstacles and provides timely feedback. Built with accessibility and affordability in mind, SafeStep promotes confidence and autonomy in everyday navigation.
